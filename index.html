Gather

A conceptual app designed to help people with food allergies cook delicious and safe meals with ease

This was my first group project in our bootcamp class- working with a team of designers is so lovely. We each brought different strengths to the work, and I was able to learn more about what mine are- namely, prioritizing, generating ideas, and stakeholder buy-in and presentation.

Introduction

While this was a far cry from our initial ideal of a gamified cooking instructional app, Gather did a better job of meeting our user's needs. Our final design was clean, beautiful, and, most importantly, useful to people.

Problem

People with food allergies have a difficult time finding safe and delicious recipes. The solution? Gather! An app that saves a user's dietary information and only shows them recipes that are safe to cook and eat.

Research

During the empathize phase of the project, I conducted user interviews to build new personas and to inform the design. Together with the team, we prepared an interview script with open ended questions, focuing on our target audience's values, motivations, and daily routines. In two days, I recruited and interviewed three users remotely. We referenced our user interview findings throughout the entire rest of the design process

What were those findings, you ask? Well, our initial idea was a gameified cooking app that would help new chefs feel confident in the kitchen. Our research, however, led us to an important insight. People who don't cook...don't want to cook. It's not because they can't or don't know how. They like their life of takeout and don't want it to change!

However, we did discover a user group with an unmet need through our research - people with food allergies constantly feel left out of delicious home cooked meals with friends, and often struggle to find easy, delicious meals that meet their restrictions. We'd found our user and our problem. Now, we just had to develop a solution.

Iteration

User Persona

Meet Erin Richardson! Our user persona borne from hours of research and analysis.

Each team member drew paper prototypes of our initial, gut-reaction drafts of what our app could look like. We compiled each into a FigJam file and took the best pieces from each person's drafts. We then created a mid-fidelity prototype and began running user testing. We also drafted a user scenario, journey-map, and user flow to ensure our mid-fidelity prototype would work as smoothly as possible and be responsive to user needs.

From our mid-fidelity user testing, we learned some key takeaways. We had some functionality issues. We also had some opportunity to iterate and add important features.

Prototype

We prioritized some key changes after user testing. First, we updated the search experience. We also added reviews for the user to read. We improved the dietary needs selection process by allowing a user to toggle on and off when choosing their allergies. We also provided more transparency to the user about how their dietary selections are used to enhance their experience within the app.

Next Steps

We were proud of our final product, but there's always more to be done. Below were our proposed next steps to improve user experience.

Further develop the recipe catalog

Our initial design was about functionality, but the app won't become really useful until there is a larger offering and variety of recipes.

Create a Settings Page

We wanted to make sure that the app stored people's preferences, but we also want them to be able to change those easily! A more fleshed out settings page will allow for that to happen.

Increase functionality of the Favorites page

Our initial version was quite basic. We have big plans for how to improve it.

Expand offerings into more restricted diets

Gather currently focuses on food common food allergies, but we plan to expand into restrictive diets like Kosher, Vegan, or Pescatarian.
